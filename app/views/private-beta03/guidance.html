Overview

Clinicians can be asked to provide medical evidence on an SR1 form to support a benefit claim made under the Special Rules.

The Special Rules allow people nearing the end of life to:

  • get faster, easier access to certain benefits
  • get higher payments for certain benefits
  • avoid a medical assessment

Use this service to tell the Department of Work and Pensions (DWP) that you have a patient who:

  • has a progressive disease
  • you would not be surprised if they were to live for less than 12 months

Who can send an SR1 form

To send an SR1 form, you must be a:

  • GP
  • hospital consultant
  • specialist doctor
  • hospice doctor
  • senior specialist nurse (such as a clinical nurse specialist, advanced nurse practitioner or similar)

If you are a GP or GMC registered consultant, you can claim a fee for completing an SR1 form, which will be paid into your bank or building society account.

What you will need

The SR1 form asks for medical evidence of:

  • diagnosis and other relevant conditions
  • date of diagnosis
  • date that they have met the criteria under Special Rules
  • clinical features that inicare a severe progressive condition
  • a treatment plan

You do not need to give a specific prognosis. Use language that you would typically use when communicating with other clinicians.

You will also need to tell us the patient’s:

  • name
  • date of birth
  • address
We can process the patient's benefit claim faster if you include their National Insurance number.

Send an SR1 form

You can return the SR1 form online, by email or by post.

Send online

The digital send an SR1 form service allows you to complete the form online. This means the form arrives instantly, reducing the patient's waiting time.

The online service is currently only available to NHS England.

If you are registered with NHS Scotland, NHS Wales or Health and Social Care in Northern Ireland, you can still send an SR1 form by email or send it by post.

Start now

Send by email

To get an electronic version of the SR1 form and the fee form, you will need to send a request from a secure email address with your:

  • surgery, hospital, or office address
  • phone number

Secure email addresses end in:

  • @nhs.net
  • @nhs.uk
  • @nhs.scot
  • @nhs.wales.uk
  • @gov.uk

The SR1 can be saved to your device. You do not need to sign either of the forms.

Email the completed SR1 forms to form.e-sr1@dwp.gov.uk.

Do not encrypt any part of the email. Any email that does not come from a secure email account will be automatically deleted.

Send by post

You can post the completed SR1 form and fee form to this address:

Personal Independence Payment (10)

Mail Handling Site A

Wolverhampton

WV98 1AE

You must:

  • sign the completed form
  • post the original copy
  • use a separate envelope for each patient's form

We cannot accept a printed copy of the electronic SR1 form.

Do not staple any documents together.

Ordering paper SR1 forms

You can order paper SR1 forms using the DWP order form (opens in a new tab).

Your order should be sent with a cover letter on headed paper. Include a list of clinicians who may complete the SR1 form in the letter.

Email the order form and cover letter from a secure email address to dwp@hhglobal.com.

You can also fax them to HH Global Limited on 0300 373 0125.

If you have any questions about this process, call the HH Global Limited helpline on 0333 016 9656.
